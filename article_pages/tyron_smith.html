Tyron Smith: The Last Ride?

Tyron Smith, at one point, was the most dominant offensive linemen in the NFL. His combination of size, strength, and athleticism has made him a cornerstone of the Cowboys' offensive line for the past decade. However, his career has been plagued by injuries in recent years, raising questions about his future with the team.

Smith was drafted by the Cowboys in the first round of the 2011 NFL Draft, and quickly established himself as one of the best left tackles in the league. He earned six consecutive Pro Bowl selections from 2013 to 2018, and was named a First-Team All-Pro twice during that span. Unfortunately, injuries have limited his playing time in recent years, with Smith playing in just two games in 2020, eleven games in 2021 and four games in 2022.

With Smith's injury history and his advancing age (he'll be 32 at the start of the 2023 NFL season), there are concerns about his future with the team. The Cowboys have already started planning for the future at the left tackle position, drafting Tyler Smith in the first round of the 2022 NFL Draft.

Despite the concerns about his future with the team, there's no denying Smith's importance to the Cowboys' success. When healthy, he's one of the best left tackles in the league, and his presence on the field gives Dak Prescott the time he needs to make plays downfield. The Cowboys will be hoping that Smith can stay healthy in 2023 and continue to anchor their offensive line.
