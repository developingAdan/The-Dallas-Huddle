Donovan Wilson: The Dallas Bull

Donovan Wilson has quickly become a standout player on the Dallas Cowboys' defense. The safety's journey to the NFL was not an easy one, but his hard work and perseverance have paid off.

Wilson grew up in Shreveport, Louisiana, and attended Woodlawn High School. He played football at Texas A&M, where he was a three-year starter and team captain his senior year. Despite being an honorable mention All-SEC selection in 2018, Wilson was not drafted until the sixth round of the 2019 NFL Draft.

Wilson's rookie season was spent mostly on special teams, but he did see some action on defense, finishing the year with 11 tackles and two passes defensed. However, it was his second season in 2020 that truly put him on the map. Wilson became a starting safety for the Cowboys and recorded 71 tackles, two interceptions, and three forced fumbles in 14 games.

Wilson's performance in this past season earned him the respect and praise of his teammates and coaches, who recognized his hard work and dedication. His ability to make plays in the passing game, and especially his physicality as a run defender, made him a valuable asset to the Cowboys' defense. His level of tenacity and energy brings something to the Cowboys defense in which they haven't had since I started watching in '09. You can *feel* the way he tackles through the TV screen. It's extremely fun to watch, to say the least.

As the Cowboys look to bounce back from a disappointing end to the 2022 season, Wilson will be a key player to watch in 2023 and beyond. With his work ethic and talent, there's no doubt that Wilson has a bright future in the NFL. Cowboys fans should be excited to watch him continue to grow and develop as a leader and player in the coming years.
