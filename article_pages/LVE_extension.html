Leighton Vander-Esch signs 2-year extension

TThe Dallas Cowboys announced they signed linebacker Leighton Vander-Esch to a two-year contract extension, keeping him with the team through the 2024 NFL season. While some fans may have been surprised by the move, I would say that it's a smart one for the Cowboys.

Vander-Esch has proven to be a reliable and serviceable middle linebacker for the Cowboys, when healthy. He's been particularly effective when playing under the guidance of defensive coordinator Dan Quinn. During Quinn's first year in Dallas in 2021, Vander Esch recorded 77 total tackles, 1 sack, and 1 interception in 17 games played.

The concern with Vander-Esch, however, is his health. He's had significant neck injuries in the past, and during this past season, that you hope don't worsen. But of course, football is a sport of heavy attrition. When he's on the field, he's a valuable asset to the Cowboys' defense.

The two-year extension is a good compromise for both Vander Esch and the Cowboys. It gives Vander-Esch a chance to prove that he can stay healthy and continue to be an effective player for the team, while also allowing the Cowboys some flexibility in their future roster decisions.

Overall, the signing of Vander-Esch is a solid move for the Cowboys. He's a proven player who has shown he can thrive in Quinn's defensive scheme. And if he can stay healthy, he'll be a valuable contributor to the Cowboys' defense for the next few seasons.
